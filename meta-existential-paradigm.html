Meta Existential Paradigm
The Meta Existential Paradigm is a framework for exploring the nature of existence.
  • Reality: What is truly there.
  • Illusion: Ways in which perception can deceive us or give falsehoods.
  • Self: The experience of being, identity, and our consciousness.
  • Other: Everything outside self, other minds, objects, external world.
  • Everything: The total of all that exists, the universe as a whole.
  • Nothing: The concept of non-existence, absence, or void.
  1. Existentialism explores meaning and freedom in a world without purpose.
  2. Phenomenology explores how reality appears to our consciousness.
  3. Idealism and Realism question whether reality is mental or independent.
  4. Absurdism and Nihilism question the possibility of meaning.
